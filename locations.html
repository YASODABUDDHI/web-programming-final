Dashboard Locations About Us

Adam's Peak (Sri Pada)

Adam's Peak, known as Sri Pada, is a sacred mountain with a distinctive conical shape, revered by various religions. The pilgrimage to the summit, especially to witness the sunrise, is a spiritual and visually stunning experience.

Arugam Bay

Arugam Bay is a surfer's paradise, known for its fantastic waves and laid-back beach vibe. This coastal area also offers beautiful scenery and a gateway to explore the nearby Kumana National Park.

Bentota

Bentota is a seaside town celebrated for its golden beaches, clear waters, and luxurious resorts. It's an ideal spot for water sports and leisure, with a picturesque lagoon adding to its charm.

Colombo

Colombo, Sri Lanka's bustling capital, blends rich colonial heritage with modern urban landscapes. It's a city of contrasts, offering vibrant street markets, high-end shopping, and a variety of cultural and historical sites.

Colombo City

As the commercial heart of Sri Lanka, Colombo City is a blend of old and new, with colonial buildings standing alongside contemporary architecture. The city is a cultural melting pot, offering a rich culinary scene and vibrant nightlife.

Dambulla Cave Temple

Dambulla Cave Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage site, features a complex of Buddhist cave temples with ancient murals and statues. It's a testament to Sri Lanka's rich religious art and history.

Ella

Ella, nestled in the mist-shrouded hills of Sri Lanka, is known for its breathtaking views, hiking trails, and the famous Nine Arch Bridge. It's a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Galle

Galle, with its iconic Dutch fort, cobbled streets, and colonial architecture, exudes a historic charm. It's a cultural hub with art galleries, shops, and cafes set against the backdrop of the Indian Ocean.

Hikkaduwa

Hikkaduwa is famous for its coral gardens, sandy beaches, and lively nightlife. It's a popular destination for snorkeling, diving, and enjoying the relaxed beach atmosphere.

Horton Plains National Park

Horton Plains National Park offers a unique ecosystem with cloud forests and grasslands. The park's highlights include the famous World's End, a sheer cliff with dramatic views, and the beautiful Baker's Falls.

Kanthale (Kan)

Kanthale, known for its impressive reservoir and rich history, is a testament to ancient engineering marvels. The serene surroundings offer a peaceful retreat and opportunities for bird watching.

Kandy

Kandy, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a city of historical and cultural significance. Home to the Temple of the Tooth Relic, traditional dances, and the Royal Botanical Gardens, Kandy is a blend of history and scenic beauty.

Matale

Matale is renowned for its spice gardens and rich cultural heritage. The town offers an insight into Sri Lanka's spice industry and features historical sites like the Aluvihara Rock Temple.

Mirissa

Mirissa, a small town on the south coast, is famous for its stunning crescent beach, whale watching tours, and vibrant nightlife. It's a perfect spot for relaxation and marine adventures.

Mirissa Beach

Mirissa Beach is an idyllic destination for sunbathing, surfing, and enjoying the tropical charm. The beach's laid-back atmosphere makes it an ideal location for a serene beach experience.

Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya, known as 'Little England', is famed for its cool climate, tea plantations, and colonial-era architecture. The town offers picturesque landscapes and is a gateway to Horton Plains National Park.

Sigiriya

Sigiriya, or Lion Rock, is an ancient rock fortress and a remarkable feat of ancient urban planning. It features water gardens, frescoes, and a unique mirror wall, making it a must-visit historical and archaeological site.

Tangalle

Tangalle is known for its palm-fringed beaches, secluded coves, and tranquil atmosphere. The town is a perfect escape for those seeking a peaceful beach experience away from the crowds.

Udawalawe National Park

Udawalawe National Park is a prime location for elephant spotting and offers a glimpse into Sri Lanka's diverse wildlife. The park's reservoir attracts various species, providing an authentic safari experience.

Unawatuna

Unawatuna, with its crescent-shaped beach and turquoise waters, is ideal for snorkeling and swimming. The beach town is also known for its vibrant nightlife and charming beachside cafes and restaurants.

Yala National Park

Yala National Park is Sri Lanka's most famous wildlife park. It's home to a variety of animals, including leopards, elephants, and crocodiles, and offers exciting safari experiences through its vast wilderness.

Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an ancient city famed for its well-preserved ruins that depict early Sri Lankan civilization. Once the capital of Sri Lanka, it holds great significance for Buddhist pilgrims and history enthusiasts

Yapahuwa

Yapahuwa, located in Sri Lanka's Wayamba province, is known for its impressive ancient Rock Fortress Complex. Though less renowned among tourists, it's considered a significant archaeological site, rivaling the more famous rock fortresses in historical importance.

Jaffna

Jaffna, or Yaalpanam, is situated at the northern tip of Sri Lanka and is one of the oldest inhabited locations in Southeast Asia. Predominantly Tamil-speaking, it's a cultural hub, blending a rich heritage with modern Sri Lankan life.

Nagadeepa

Nagadeepa, also known as Nainativu, is a small but significant island in the Gulf of Mannar, accessible only by boat. This serene island, enveloped in religious and historical importance, is a site where Buddha is believed to have visited, according to the Mahavamsa.
